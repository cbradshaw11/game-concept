# Progression Tracks
## The Long Walk

Date: 2026-03-09
Status: Drafted for selection

## Track A: Story-First (Finite Arc + Post-Game)

### Goal
Ship an MVP with a clear ending in Ring 3, then unlock an endless replay mode.

### Player Promise
- "I can finish the main journey in 8 to 12 hours."
- "If I want more, post-game gives infinite challenge loops."

### Structure
1. Act 1 (Ring 1: Inner)
- Learn combat and extraction.
- Recover first map shard.

2. Act 2 (Ring 2: Mid)
- Enemy behaviors become mixed and aggressive.
- Recover second map shard.

3. Act 3 (Ring 3: Outer)
- Highest risk runs and environmental hazards.
- Beat the Warden and extract with the Artifact (or Cure/Key).

4. Ending
- Credits trigger after successful extraction with Artifact.
- Safe zone state changes to reflect completion.

5. Post-Game Unlock: Frontier Mode
- Remix contracts across rings.
- Add modifiers and elite affixes.
- Chase mastery, cosmetics, and leaderboard-style goals.

### Pros
- Strong motivation and pacing.
- Easy to test completion criteria.
- Cleaner marketing message for MVP launch.

### Risks
- Requires enough narrative delivery quality to land the ending.
- Some players may want infinite mode sooner.

## Track B: Replay-First (Open-Ended From Day 1)

### Goal
Ship quickly with deep run replayability and no explicit campaign ending.

### Player Promise
- "Every run can push farther and improve my build."
- "There is always another optimization target."

### Structure
1. Ring progression is cyclical
- Players push, extract, upgrade, repeat.
- New affixes and challenge layers unlock with rank.

2. Long-term goals
- Weapon mastery tiers.
- Contract streaks.
- Relic collection and perfect runs.

3. Narrative style
- Lore is optional and discovery-based.
- No hard ending sequence.

### Pros
- Lower narrative production pressure.
- Fast content reuse through modifiers and procedural mix.

### Risks
- Weaker short-term motivation for broad audience.
- Harder to define "done" for MVP.
- Can feel grindy if progression pacing is off.

## Recommendation
Choose Track A for MVP:
- Better scope control.
- Better testability and release criteria.
- Supports Track B anyway through Frontier Mode after credits.
